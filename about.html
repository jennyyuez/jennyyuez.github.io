How I work

Design that scales

I don't design for one user in isolation. I think about how a single decision ripples across a hundred different contexts — different roles, different devices, different levels of technical literacy. That systems-first thinking shapes everything I do.

Empathy as precision
Understanding users isn't a soft skill — it's the most precise tool a designer has. Especially when those users are working in high-stakes physical environments where bad UX has real consequences.
Modular thinking
I build systems, not screens. Components, patterns, and IA diagrams that give teams a shared language and reduce the cost of change as products evolve.
Ownership over output
I care about what ships, not just what I design. That means staying close to engineering, triaging issues, monitoring quality in production, and iterating based on what users actually do.

"I'm not interested in staying in a lane. I'm interested in whatever it takes to make the product better for the people using it."

— Jenny Yue
