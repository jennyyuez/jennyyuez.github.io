Designer × AI

The handoff is dead.
I design it — and build it.

AI changed what one designer can ship. I use Claude as a daily collaborator — not to replace craft, but to extend it past the Figma frame and into production.

⌘

I ship my own features. Front-end improvements I design go from mockup to merged — built hands-on with Claude Code, reviewed and released with the engineering team.

✦

I build agents. Designing the prompts, tools, and guardrails that turn repetitive product work into something an agent handles — so the team can spend time on judgment, not busywork.

▲

I prototype in real code. When a flow is too complex for a clickthrough, I build the real thing — testing with users on working software instead of smoke and mirrors.

❋

Including this site. Designed by me, built in a pairing session with Claude — the same way I work every day.

Two hats, one outcome

Designer first.
Product manager when it counts.

My title says both because my work does both. The craft makes the product feel right; the product thinking makes sure it's the right product.

As a designer

I sweat the craft

  • End-to-end product design — research, flows, prototypes, polished UI across web and mobile
  • Design systems people actually adopt — components, patterns, Storybook, WCAG 2.1
  • Information architecture that makes complex products legible to the whole team
  • User interviews and usability testing as a habit, not a phase
  • Visual craft from a background in brand, illustration, and interior architecture
&

As a product manager

I own the outcome

  • Roadmaps and quarterly planning co-owned with founders — strategy translated into shippable work
  • Built the foundation that took a product to $1M ARR
  • Stabilised the module behind 70% of support issues while onboarding a new team
  • Prioritisation under real constraints — deadlines, migrations, competing teams
  • Hiring and mentoring — led recruitment of 10 designers and grew them toward product leadership

A bit about me

A non-linear path,
on purpose

I started in interior architecture, moved through graphic design and brand, and found my home in product. I never explain that away — it's what makes me different. I see problems spatially, think in systems before screens, and bring a level of visual craft to product work that comes from years of caring how things look and feel.

The thread through all of it: interactive, inclusive design — products that work for everyone, built by teams that enjoy building them.
