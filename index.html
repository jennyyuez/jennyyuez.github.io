A bit about me

Design is how I
make sense of
the world

I started in interior architecture, moved into graphic design, and found my home in product. That non-linear path isn't something I explain away — it's what makes me different. I see problems spatially, think about systems before screens, and bring a level of craft to product work that comes from years of caring about how things look and feel.

Full background & experience
1
Empathy as precision
Understanding users deeply isn't a soft skill — it's the most precise tool a designer has. Especially when those users are working in high-stakes environments.
2
Systems before screens
I build components, patterns, and IA that give teams a shared language. Good systems reduce the cost of change and make the whole team move faster.
3
Ownership over output
I care about what ships, not just what I design. That means staying close to engineering, triaging real issues, and iterating on what users actually do.
4
Scale thinking
I don't design for one user. I ask how a single decision plays out across a hundred different contexts — roles, devices, levels of technical literacy.
